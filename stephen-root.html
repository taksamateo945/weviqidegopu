Stephen Root is an actor/voice-over artist from America. He is well-known for his roles as Jimmy James in the television sitcom “NewsRadio” and Milton Waddams in the 1999 black comedy film “Office Space.” He is also known for his voice role as Bill Dauterive and Buck Strickland in the animated sitcom “King of the Hill.”

Over three decades, the sixty-eight-year-old veteran actor has made over seventy films and ninety television series. He has also done voice roles for fifteen movies and fifty-one television series.

Early Life

He was born to Leona Estelle and Rolland Clairon on 17th November 1951 in Sarasota, Florida. His father worked as a project superintendent/site manager on several engineering projects. Due to his father’s job, the family frequently relocated.

Education: School/College

Stephen Root completed his high school studies at Vero Beach High School in Florida. He then enrolled in the University of Florida and gained an associate degree. He also enrolled in the University’s BFA program but left in the final semester because of his acting career. He later completed the BFA degree, and the college recognized him as one of the alumni in 2008.
