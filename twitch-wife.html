Twitch Wife: The Tragic Death of Stephen tWitch Boss and His Legacy

Posted by Martina Birk on Saturday, February 17, 2024

Stephen “tWitch” Boss was a renowned dancer, DJ, and TV personality who died by suicide on December 13, 2022, at the age of 40.

He left behind his wife, Allison Holker, and their three children, Weslie, Maddox, and Zaia.

His death shocked his fans and colleagues, who remembered him as a joyful, talented, and generous person.

In this article, we will explore his life, career, marriage, and estate, as well as how his wife and kids are coping with the loss.

Who was Stephen “tWitch” Boss?

Stephen “tWitch” Boss was born on September 29, 1982, in Montgomery, Alabama.

He started dancing at the age of 8 and developed a passion for hip-hop and street styles.

He auditioned for the third season of So You Think You Can Dance in 2007 but was cut in the Vegas callbacks.

Stephen returned for the fourth season in 2008 and made it to the finale, finishing as the runner-up behind Joshua Allen.

He became one of the most popular contestants on the show and was invited back as an All-Star in several seasons.

He also appeared in several movies and TV shows, such as:

  • Stomp the Yard 2: Homecoming
  • Step Up 3D
  • Step Up Revolution
  • Magic Mike XXL
  • Bones
  • The Ellen DeGeneres Show
  • Disney’s Shake It Up

Stephen was best known as the DJ and co-executive producer of The Ellen DeGeneres Show since 2014, where he entertained the audience with his music and dance moves.

He also hosted Disney’s Fairy Tale Weddings with his wife Allison Holker.

He won two Primetime Emmy Awards for Outstanding Choreography in 2015 and 2017 for his work on So You Think You Can Dance and The Ellen DeGeneres Show.

Stephen also received several nominations for his performances and routines.

How did he meet Allison Holker?

Allison Holker is also a dancer, choreographer, and actress who competed in the second season of So You Think You Can Dance in 2006 and placed in the top 8.

She later returned as an All-Star in various seasons and also became a pro dancer on Dancing with the Stars.

She met Stephen “tWitch” Boss on the set of So You Think You Can Dance in 2010 when they were both All-Stars.

They started dating soon after and got engaged in January 2013.

They got married on December 10, 2013, at a vineyard owned by Nigel Lythgoe, the creator of So You Think You Can Dance.

Allison and Stephen had a beautiful wedding ceremony that featured their own choreographed dance routine.

They shared a love for dance and often posted videos of themselves dancing together on social media platforms like TikTok and Instagram.

They also collaborated on various projects, such as hosting Disney’s Fairy Tale Weddings and starring in an HGTV show called Design Star: Next Gen.

Allison and Stephen had two children together: son Maddox Laurel Boss, born on March 27, 2016, and daughter Zaia Boss, born on November 6, 2019.

Stephen also adopted Allison’s daughter from a previous relationship, Weslie Renae Fowler, born on May 26, 2008.

What happened to Stephen “tWitch” Boss?

On December 13, 2022, Stephen “tWitch” Boss was found dead at a motel in Los Angeles due to a self-inflicted gunshot wound to the head.

He had checked into the motel the day before using an Uber and turned his phone on aeroplane mode so no one could contact him or trace his location.

He left behind a suicide note that hinted at some past challenges and struggles that he was facing but did not reveal any specific details.

His death came as a shock to his family, friends, fans, and colleagues who had no idea that he was suffering from any mental health issues.

He had always appeared to be happy, upbeat, positive, and supportive of others.

His wife Allison Holker confirmed his death in a heartbreaking statement to People magazine on December 14.

What is his estate worth and who inherits it?

According to Celebrity Net Worth, Stephen “tWitch” Boss had an estimated net worth of $5 million at the time of his death.

He earned his wealth from his various endeavours as a dancer, DJ, actor, host, and producer.

However, he did not have a will when he died, which means that his estate had to go through the probate process to determine how his assets and debts would be distributed according to the law.

In California, where he lived and died, the law states that if a person dies without a will and is survived by a spouse and children,

The spouse inherits all of the community property (the property acquired during the marriage) and one-third or one-half of the separate property (the property acquired before the marriage or by gift or inheritance), depending on the number of children.

The children inherit the remaining share of the separate property.

Therefore, Allison Holker filed a spousal property petition in February 2023 to obtain control of half of their joint estate and future earnings as his surviving spouse.

She also requested to have all shares of stock passed and confirmed from his various projects into her name.

Allison also asked for half of his company Stephen Boss Productions and the assets tied to his Goldman Sachs investment account and other ventures.

She also claimed that she was entitled to receive royalties from his appearances on SAG/AFTRA, Disney, and other entities.

A Los Angeles Superior Court judge granted her petition in April 2023.

It gave her one-half interest in 100 per cent of all shares of his company, along with one-half of the interest earned in an investment account.

She also received one-half of future earnings and royalties from entities relating to his professional pursuits as his surviving spouse.

How are Allison Holker and their kids doing now?

Allison Holker has been trying to cope with the loss of her husband and keep her family together in the aftermath of his death.

She has been receiving support from her family, friends, fans, and colleagues who have been sending her love and encouragement.

She has also been honouring his memory by continuing to dance and share her passion with others.

Allison has posted several dance videos on her Instagram and TikTok accounts since his death, showing her resilience and strength.

She has also been involved in various projects, such as :

  • hosting Design Star: Next Gen on HGTV
  • appearing on The Today Show.

 The Move With Kindness Foundation

She has also established the Move With Kindness Foundation.

It supports mental health organizations as a way to pay tribute to her husband’s legacy.

Allison said:

“We always hear, ‘Reach out to the strongest people,’ and I believe in that. But I also want the message to be that if you’re feeling low or depressed, it’s okay to lean on someone else… Trust that people are still going to see you as that light even in your darkest moments.”

She has also been focusing on her children’s well-being and happiness.

She has celebrated their birthdays, milestones, achievements, and interests with them.

Allison has also been open with them about their father’s death and how they can cope with their grief.

She said:

“I think it’s really important for kids to understand that emotions are OK, and it’s OK to feel sad, it’s OK to feel angry, it’s OK to feel happy.

And we talk about it every single day.

We talk about how we’re feeling, we talk about what we’re going through, we talk about what we miss about him, we talk about what we love about him.”

She added:

“And I think it’s really important for them to know that they’re not alone in this and that there are other people out there that are going through similar things, and that we can lean on each other for support.”

He left behind his wife Allison Holker and their three children Weslie, Maddox, and Zaia.

His death shocked his fans and colleagues who remembered him as a joyful, talented, and generous person.
