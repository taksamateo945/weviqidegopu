Brie Larson puts on busty display in beige corset and fluffy silk slippers as she attends NBA game

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Wednesday, August 7, 2024

Brie Larson looked effortlessly stylish as she attended a basketball game in a pair of fluffy silk slippers, alongside her boyfriend Elijah Allan-Blitz on New Year’s Eve.

The actress, 32, and her 34-year-old beau were sitting courtside at an NBA game between the Portland Trail Blazers and Los Angeles Lakers at the Crypto.Com Arena in Los Angeles.

The couple was first romantically linked in 2019 and eventually collaborated on a virtual reality-based project that earned them a Primetime Emmy Award and things look to be as good as ever.

Prior to becoming involved with the filmmaker, the actress was in a long-term relationship with Phantom Planet singer Alex Greenwald.

The Kong: Skull Island actress also briefly dated actors Cody Linley and John Patrick Amedori before entering into a relationship with her former partner.

Brie was first spotted kissing her current boyfriend in a Calabasas parking lot in July of 2019, although they attempted to keep things between them away from the public for an extended period of time. 

After they went public during the awards ceremony, the couple was spotted in public much more frequently.

The two eventually collaborated on The Messy Truth VR Experience, which was released last year.

Dashing: Elijah looked handsome beside her, sporting a complementary beige coat, a similar colour to the jacket that Brie wore

The virtual reality project placed viewers in several uncomfortable situations that reflect various social issues that its developers felt were in need of greater exposure and understanding.

For their efforts, Brie and Elijah, along with their collaborator Van Jones, were ultimately the recipients of the 2020 Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Original Interactive Program.

Last year, the couple spoke to Deadline and talked about how the project was meant to act as a tool for a gradual social change towards the perception of the issues that were covered.

Elijah specifically noted that virtual reality ‘has the potential to make the viewer believe what they’re seeing is real, so for us, using it to create empathy and understanding is the ultimate goal.’

Brie also expressed: ‘The project is about creating unity, not more division. When you are in the headset your brain believes that what you are seeing is real so it is important to us that their new memories are impactful but not traumatic.

‘I’m inspired to see how [The Messy Truth VR Experience] can impact others as it has impacted me,’ she added.
